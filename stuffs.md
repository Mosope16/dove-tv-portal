For a full-scale broadcast network like **Dove TV**, the foundation you've built is incredibly solid. Since Television networks deal with heavy logistics, high-stress time management, and complex hardware, here are some powerful features we could add to transform this into a master-level Operations Management System (OMS):

### 1. Equipment & Inventory Checkout (Gear Vault)
While we are tracking Studio Space and Marketing Assignments, television producers constantly need field gear. 
- **The Feature:** A module where camera operators or producers can select specific assets (e.g., *Sony FX9, Wireless Lav Mics, Lighting Kits, SD Cards*) from a database and check them out for an assignment. 
- **Why it matters:** It automatically tracks "Checked Out" vs. "In Vault", logs exactly who has what gear, flags if equipment is overdue to be returned, and requires Engineering Manager approval for highly expensive gear.

### 2. Live Broadcast & Shift Roster (The Grid)
TV Stations run on a highly volatile 24/7 tape. 
- **The Feature:** A centralized visual Calendar or Gantt Chart interface. Instead of just seeing requests in a table, Managers can view a visual timeline of the week showing exactly what is happening in Studio A vs. Studio B at any given hour.
- **Why it matters:** It allows you to drag-and-drop shifts, assigning specific `PROG_ENG` (Program Engineers) to specific time slots, ensuring no live recording is ever accidentally left unstaffed.

### 3. External Guest Clearance (The Green Room module)
You likely bring in external pastors, commentators, or guests for recordings who don't have `@dovetv` accounts.
- **The Feature:** An external, unauthenticated ticket system. When a producer creates a Studio Booking, they can generate a secure shareable link for their guest. 
- **Why it matters:** The guest clicks it via WhatsApp, fills out their Name, signs a digital Media Release Form, and receives an Automated Security QR Code to show the front gate to get into the building smoothly.

### 4. Technical Fault Reporting (Ticketing System)
In a studio, things break. Cables fail, lighting grids blow fuses, and switchers crash.
- **The Feature:** A dedicated "Fault Report" or "IT Ticket" tab where floor staff can instantly shoot an alert to the Engineering Managers: *"Microphone 3 in Studio A is outputting static."*
- **Why it matters:** It keeps a running tally of broken gear that needs maintenance, routing requests specifically to technical staff so producers aren't left stranded on set with broken tech.

### 5. Automated Email/SMS Reminders
- **The Feature:** Hooking into an email or SMS gateway natively through Next.js.
- **Why it matters:** 1 hour before a Studio Recording is set to begin, the Presenter and Producer automatically get a text message to their phone or an email saying: *"Reminder: Your studio session for [Program Name] begins in 1 hour in Studio A."*
